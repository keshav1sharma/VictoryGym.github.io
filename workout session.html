  • At home workout

    Wheather you're a seasoned expert or new to strength training, working out at home is a great option when you can't get to the gym or need a change of pace. The at to home workouts below require a limited amount of equipment. Plus, some of the movements can be subsituted for bodyweight exercises in which you use your body's own weight as resistence.

    • Equipment required: flat to weight bench, appropriate adjustable dumbbells based on your level of experience.
    Rest intervals: 60 to 90 seconds

    Day1: legs, shoulders and abs

    • Legs: dumbbell squats to 3 sets of 6 to 8 reps
    • Shoulders: standing shoulder press to 3sets of 6 to 8 reps
    • Legs: dumbbell lunge to 2 sets of 8 to 10 reps
    • Hamstrings: romanian dumbbell upright rows to 2 sets of 6 to 8 reps
    • Shoulders: lateral raises to 3 sets of 8 to 10 reps
    • Calves: seated calf raises to 4 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Abs: crunches with legs elevated to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps

    Days2: chest and back

    • Chest: dumbbell bench press or floor press to 3 sets of 6 to 8 reps
    • Back: dumbbbell bent over rows to 3 sets of 6 to 8 reps
    • Chest: dumbbell fly to 3 sets of 8 to 10 reps
    • Back: one to arm dumbbell rows to 3 sets of 6 to 8 reps
    • Chest: pushups to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Back/chest: dumbbell pullovers to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps

    Day 3: arms and abs

    • Biceps: alternating bicep curls to 3 sets of 8 to 10 reps per arm
    • Triceps:overhead tricep extensions to 3 sets of 8 to 10 reps
    • Biceps: seated dumbbell curls to 2 sets of 10 to 12 reps per arm
    • Triceps: bench dips to 2 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Biceps: concentration curls to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Triceps: dumbbell kickbacks to 3 sets of 8 to 10 reps per arm
    • Abs: planks to 3 sets of 30 to second holds

  • Beginner's workout

    Starting out in the gym can seem intimidating, but with proper guidance the process becomes more approachable to and even invigorating. As a beginner, you can progress very quickly because almost any exercise promotes muscle and strength gains. Still, it's important to avoid overexertion, which can lead to injuries or decreased performance. This workout routine has you in the gym 3 days per week (such as Monday, Wednesday, and Friday), with full to body sessions completed each day. This allows you to get used to new movements, focus on proper form, and take time to recover.

    • Equipment required: fully to equipped gym
    Rest periods: 90 to 180 seconds for main movements, 60 to 90 seconds for accessories
    Intensity: Select a weight that allows you to complete the prescribed reps while leaving about two solid reps in the tank.

    Day 1: full body

    • Legs: barbell back squats to 5 sets of 5 reps
    • Chest: flat barbell bench press to 5 set of 5 reps
    • Back: seated cable rows to 4 sets of 6 to 8 reps
    • Shoulders: seated dumbbell shoulder press to 4 sets of 6 to 8 reps
    • Triceps: cable rope tricep pushdowns to 3 sets of 8 to 10 reps
    • Shoulders: lateral raises to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Calves: seated calf raises to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Abs: planks to 3 sets of 30 second holds

    Day 2: full body

    • Legs: leg press to 5 sets of 5 reps
    • Back: T to bar rows to 3 sets of 6 to 8 reps
    • Chest: machine or dumbbell chest fly to 3 sets of 6 to 8 reps
    • Shoulders: one to arm dumbbell shoulder press to 3 sets of 6 to 8 reps
    • Triceps: dumbbell or machine tricep extensions to 3 sets of 8 to 10 reps
    • Shoulders: cable or dumbbell front raises to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Calves: seated calf raises to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Abs: decline crunches to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps

    Day 3: full body

    • Legs: leg press to 5 sets of 5 reps
    • Back: T to bar rows to 3 sets of 6 to 8 reps
    • Chest: machine or dumbbell chest fly to 3 sets of 6 to 8 reps
    • Shoulders: one to arm dumbbell shoulder press to 3 sets of 6 to 8 rep
    • Triceps: dumbbell or machine tricep extensions to 3 sets of 8 to 10 reps
    • Shoulders: cable or dumbbell front raises to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Calves: seated calf raises to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Abs: decline crunches to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps

  • Intermediate workout

    After working hard in the gym for several months, it's time to step your training up a notch to keep your gains coming. At this point, you should have good exercise technique and be able to handle more weight on the bar. This 4 to day to per to week intermediate program increases reps and sets to stimulate new muscle growth. When they become too easy, you can gradually add more weight or more reps/sets. If done right, you can follow this routine for several years until you reach an advanced level.

    • Equipment required: fully to equipped gym
    Rest intervals: 90 to 180 seconds for main movements, 60 to 90 seconds for accessories
    Intensity: Select a weight that allows you to complete the prescribed reps while leaving about two solid reps in the tank. To increase intensity, go to your limit on the last set.

    Day 1: upper body

    • Chest: flat barbell bench press to 4 sets of 6 to 8 reps
    • Back: bent over barbell rows to 3 sets of 6 to 8 reps
    • Shoulders: seated dumbbell press to 3 sets of 8 to 10 reps
    • Chest/triceps: dips to 3 sets of 8 to 10 reps
    • Back: pullups or lat pulldowns to 3 sets of 8 to 10 reps
    • Triceps/chest: flying dumbbell tricep extensions to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Biceps: incline dumbbell curls to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps

    Day 2: lower body

    • Legs: barbell back squats to 4 sets of 6 to 8 reps
    • Legs: leg press to 3 sets of 8 to 10 reps
    • Quadriceps: seated leg extensions to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Quadriceps: dumbbell or barbell walking lunges to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps (no videos)
    • Calves: calf press on leg press to 4 sets of 12 to 15 reps
    • Abs: decline crunches to 4 sets of 12 to 15 reps

    Day 3: upper body

    • Shoulders: overhead press to 4 sets of 6 to 8 reps
    • Chest: incline dumbbell bench press to 3 sets of 8 to 10 reps
    • Back: one to arm cable rows to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Shoulders: cable lateral raises to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Rear deltoids/traps: face pulls to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Traps: dumbbell shrugs to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Triceps: seated overhead tricep extensions to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Biceps: machine preacher curls to 3 sets of 12 to 15 reps

    Day 4: lower body

    • Back/hamstrings: barbell deadlift to 4 sets of 6 reps
    • Glutes: barbell hip thrusts to 3 sets of 8 to 10 reps
    • Hamstrings: Romanian dumbbell deadlifts to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Hamstrings: lying leg curls to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Calves: seated calf raises to 4 sets of 12 to 15 reps
    • Abs: leg raises on Roman chair to 4 sets of 12 to 15 reps

  • Advanced workout

    Additional volume (sets and reps) and intensity (weight on the bar) is essential for advanced gym to goers to keep gaining muscle. Keep in mind that this routine should not be attempted unless you've been training consistently for 2 or more years. While the muscle gains won't come as fast as they did when you were a beginner, there's still room for significant progress at this stage. This gruelling workout routine has you in the gym 6 days per week with 1 rest day in between.

    • Equipment required: fully to equipped gym
    Rest periods: 90 to 180 seconds for main movements, 60 to 90 seconds for accessories
    Intensity: Select a weight that allows you to complete the prescribed reps while leaving about 2 solid reps in the tank. To increase intensity, go to failure on the last set.
    Supersets: Complete the initial set of the first movement immediately followed by the second movement. Repeat until all designated reps and sets are complete.

    Pull A

    • Back/hamstrings: barbell deadlift to 5 sets of 5 reps
    • Back: pullups or lat pulldowns to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Back: T to bar rows or seated cable rows to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Rear deltoids/traps: face pulls to 4 sets of 12 to 15 reps
    • Biceps: hammer curls to 4 sets of 10 to 12 reps supersetted with dumbbell shrugs 4 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Biceps: standing cable curls to 4 sets of 10 to 12 reps

    Push A

    • Chest: flat barbell bench press to 5 set of 5 reps
    • Shoulders: seated dumbbell press to 3 sets of 6 to 8 reps
    • Chest: incline dumbbell bench press to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Triceps/shoulders: tricep pushdowns to 4 sets of 10 to 12 reps supersetted with lateral raises to 4 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Chest: cable crossovers to 4 sets of 10 to 12 reps

    Legs A

    • Legs: barbell back squats to 5 sets of 5 reps
    • Hamstrings: Romanian dumbbell deadlifts to 3 sets of 6 to 8 reps
    • Legs: leg press to 3 sets of 8 to 10 reps
    • Hamstrings: lying leg curls to 4 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Calves: seated calf raises to 4 sets of 12 to 15 reps
    • Abs: decline crunches to 4 sets of 12 to 15 reps

    Pull B

    • Back: bent over barbell rows to 3 sets of 6 to 8 reps
    • Back: pull to ups (weighted if needed) to 3 sets of 8 to 10 reps
    • Back: one to arm rows to 3 sets of 8 to 10 reps
    • Lower back: hyperextensions to 4 sets of 10 to 12 reps supersetted with machine preacher curls to 4 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Traps: barbell shrugs to 4 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Biceps: standing dumbbell curls to 4 sets of 10 to 12 reps

    Push B

    • Shoulders: overhead press to 5 sets of 5 reps
    • Chest: dumbbell bench press (incline or flat) to 3 sets of 8 to 10 reps
    • Chest/triceps: dips (weighted if needed) to 4 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Shoulders: single arm cable lateral raises to 4 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Chest: machine fly to 4 sets of 10 to 12 reps
    • Triceps: overhead extensions with rope to 4 sets of 10 to 12 reps

    Legs B

    • Legs: barbell front squats to 5 sets of 5 reps
    • Hamstrings: glute ham raises to 3 sets of 8 to 10 reps
    • Legs: walking dumbbell lunges to 3 sets of 10 to 12 reps per leg
    • Quadriceps: seated leg extensions to 4 sets of 10 to 12 reps supersetted with standing calf raises to 4 sets of 12 to 15 reps
    • Abs: hanging leg raises to 4 sets of 12 to 15 reps

15 Days ABS challenge

It's not only six to pack seekers who should be paying their abs plenty of attention when they visit the gym. Strong abs are an integral part of a rock to solid core, which should be one of the goals of any fitness regime. That's because a strong core is the foundation upon which so many other things rest to whether that's good posture that helps to prevent lower back pain developing from sitting at a desk all day, or the mobility and strength required to excel in sports and other activities ranging all the way from athletics to zumba. Forming a mighty midriff will also help you perform better when doing all manner of other moves in the gym. Powerhouse lifts like barbell squats and deadlifts all demand a strong core, as do agility workouts and plyometric exercises.

30 Days HIIT challenge

HIIT involves short bursts of intense exercise alternated with low intensity recovery periods. Interestingly, it is perhaps the most time to efficient way to exercise. Typically, a HIIT workout will be 10 to 30 minutes in duration. Despite how short the workout is, it can produce health benefits similar to twice as much moderate to intensity exercise . The actual activity being performed varies but can include sprinting, biking, jumping rope, or other bodyweight exercises. For example, a HIIT workout using a stationary bike could consist of 30 seconds of cycling as fast as possible with high resistance, followed by several minutes of slow, easy cycling with low resistance. This would be considered one “round” or “repetition” of HIIT, and you would typically complete 4 to 6 reps in one workout. The specific amount of time you exercise and recover will vary based on the activity you choose and how intensely you are exercising. Regardless of how you implement this strategy, high intensity intervals should involve short periods of vigorous exercise that make your heart rate speed up. HIIT not only provides the benefits of longer to duration exercise in a much shorter amount of time but also may provide some unique health benefits.


30 Days Full Body challenge

To add as much lean muscle mass as possible over the next 28 days, while also stripping off body fat, it's important you follow this training plan as closely as possible. It's been designed to tax your major muscle groups, especially your chest and back, to radically transform how you look shirtless. The four to week plan is made up of four sessions a week, which you will ideally do on a Monday, Wednesday, Friday and Saturday. The Monday session targets your chest and back; the Wednesday one your legs and shoulders; the Friday session your chest and triceps; and the Saturday one your back and biceps. This means you'll work your major upper to body muscles either directly or indirectly twice a week, and it's this big increase in training volume that will stimulate these muscles into growing bigger quickly.

In general, we're all about the long term when it comes to fitness. Getting in shape can take a long time, and keeping yourself in shape is the work of a lifetime. 60 Days Full Body challenge

A lot of guys go into the gym, push it as hard as they can for days or weeks at a time, then slow down when they either start to feel burned out or find themselves hampered by nagging injuries. Of course, there's a better way to train to avoid muscular and central nervous system burnout. This plan is that better way and was formulated by one of the most respected individuals in the fitness industry, James Grage, co to founder and executive vice president of BPI Sports in Hollywood, FL. Grage isn't just a successful businessman, he's also a gym rat in the trenches who's been pounding the iron for decades with a shredded physique to show for it. Grage accomplished this with a program design element that elite athletes have been following forever: alternating between heavy and light workouts. Every heavy day is either followed by a light or rest day. This promotes recovery so that you don't burn yourself out with too many difficult workouts in one week.A bigger, stronger, leaner, and more injury to resistant body can be yours in just 60 days, and it all starts here.
